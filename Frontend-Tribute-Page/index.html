About Ulrich Beck

Ulrich Beck was the most well-known German sociologist and one of the most cited social scientists in the world during his lifetime.
He was born on May 15, 1944 in Stolp, Germany (now Słupsk, Poland), and grew up in Hannover.
After studying in Freiburg with a focus on law, he continued his studies of sociology, philosophy, psychology and political science at the University of Munich.
After appointments as professor at the universities of Münster (1979 - 1981) and Bamberg (1981 - 1992), he became professor of sociology at the University of Munich and also directed its Insitute of Sociology until his death.
Furthermore, he was appointed professor at the London School of Economics and Political Science as well as the French Fondation Maison des Sciences de L'Homme.
He coined concepts like Individualization, Reflexive Modernization and (World) Risk Society.
He collaborated with many other social scientists, including Anthony Giddens and his wife Elisabeth Beck-Gernsheim.
Never growing tired of asking social sciences themselves to overcome their thinking in "Containers" like national borders, he propagated a Cosmopolitan Sociology.
He died of a heart attack in Munich on January 1, 2015 at the age of 70.
